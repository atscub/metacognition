## Honest read: mixed, and the headline claim isn't supported yet.

### What the data actually shows

**FlowGuard demonstrably improves how developers feel:**
- Focus self-report: +1.6 points, p=0.003 — real effect
- Flow state duration: +4.2 min, p=0.008 — real effect

**FlowGuard does NOT demonstrate productivity improvement in this study:**
- Task completion time: p=0.14 — not significant
- Correctness: p=0.31 — not significant

Those last two are the metrics that matter most for a productivity claim. The improvements are in the right direction (−2.2 min, +3%), but statistically you can't distinguish them from noise.

### Why this matters

The tool's *premise* is productivity improvement. The *evidence* supports experience improvement. Those aren't the same thing. "Developers feel more focused" is real value, but if you market or pitch this as "FlowGuard makes developers more productive," this data doesn't back that up.

### What's probably going on

N=12 is likely underpowered for detecting small-to-medium productivity effects. A back-of-envelope power calculation: to detect a 9% reduction in completion time at 80% power, you'd likely need 30–50 participants. The study wasn't big enough to confirm or rule out real effects on the hard metrics.

### A methodological concern I should name

These were simulated coding tasks I designed — not real work. That's a validity threat. A 20-minute lab task doesn't have the same interruption pressure, context depth, or stakes as a real feature sprint. The flow state effects might be larger or smaller in real conditions. The observed-failure methodology we've used elsewhere (real failures caught by humans) would be more credible here than AI-designed tasks.

### Bottom line

| Claim | Supported? |
|---|---|
| "FlowGuard makes developers feel more focused" | Yes |
| "FlowGuard extends flow state duration" | Yes |
| "FlowGuard makes developers faster" | Not proven (underpowered) |
| "FlowGuard makes developers more accurate" | Not proven (underpowered) |
| "75% of developers want to keep using it" | Yes, but likely driven by the subjective experience |

**What I'd recommend**: Don't claim productivity improvement yet. Either rerun with more participants (~40+) targeting the hard metrics specifically, or reframe FlowGuard's value prop around the thing it actually proved — protecting focus and extending flow state — and let the productivity hypothesis remain an open question pending a larger study.
