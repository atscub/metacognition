**What do I actually know vs. pattern-match?**
- The "10% of what we read" figure is widely cited — but this is a known zombie statistic. It traces back to misattributions of Edgar Dale's "Cone of Experience," which never contained those percentages. Dale himself didn't claim them.
- Ebbinghaus forgetting curve research (actual empirical work): passive reading leads to ~50% forgetting within an hour, ~70% within 24 hours — but this is for arbitrary material without prior schema.
- Schema theory (Bartlett): retention is heavily anchored to prior knowledge — experts retain far more than novices reading the same text.
- Retrieval practice research (Roediger, Karpicke): testing after reading dramatically improves retention vs. re-reading.

**What would change my mind?** A well-powered RCT with standardized material, standardized delay, and standardized recall test. I'm not aware of one that yields a clean universal figure.

**Crux of uncertainty:** The question is underspecified — retention of what material, tested when, by whom, with what prior knowledge, and measured how?
