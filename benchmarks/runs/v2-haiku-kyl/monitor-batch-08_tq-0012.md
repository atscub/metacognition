## Honest Answer

Yes, you can be a "non-Christian Christian" — this describes people who identify with Christianity culturally, historically, or ethically but reject core theological tenets.

**What this actually means** (the semantic resolution):
- **Cultural Christian**: Raised Christian, participates in Christian traditions/holidays, may not believe in God
- **Christian atheist**: Follows Jesus's ethical teaching while rejecting supernatural claims
- **Christian nominal**: Institutionally registered (baptized) but non-practicing and non-believing
- **Secular Christian**: Values Christian philosophy without religious faith

This isn't incoherent — it's acknowledging that identity is multidimensional. You can be "X in some dimensions, not-X in others."
