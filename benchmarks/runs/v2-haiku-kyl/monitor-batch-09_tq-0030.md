## Answer

**Recordings that are generally restricted/illegal in the UK:**

1. **Interception of communications** — Recording phone calls or intercepting messages without proper authority or consent (RIPA 2000)
2. **Private conversations without consent** — Recording someone in a private setting (home, office) without their knowledge/consent
3. **Unlawful surveillance** — Recording with intent to violate privacy rights
4. **Breach of workplace confidentiality** — Recording conversations in workplaces without proper notice/consent (varies by employment context)

**However:**
- Recording in **public places** often has fewer restrictions
- Recording **yourself** (one-party consent) may be legal in some contexts
- **Legal exemptions** exist for law enforcement, court-ordered surveillance, and specific authorized purposes
- Rules vary by **employment context**, **location type**, and **type of communication**
